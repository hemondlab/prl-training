PRL assessment

Red Flags

Veins running alongside the rim are difficult to distinguish from a PRL rim
The hypointense rim should be spatially aligned with either the T2-FLAIR or T1-hypointense lesion edge.
PRL criteria are not designed for lesions that are part of a large confluent lesion complex. Occasionally an embededded T1 hypointensity can be convincingly correlated with hypointense phase edges.
Hypointense rims should be visible for at least 2/3 of the lesion circumference, excluding cortical or ependymal/ventricular borders
PRL rims should be visible on at least 2 adjacent slices. If the rim is only visible on a single slice, it is very unlikely to represent a PRL.

Yellow Flags

Small paramagnetic/diamagnetic structures, e.g. iron-laden ferritin or hemosiderin dots, veins and myelin debris.
Small lesions (<3mm in diameter) are difficult to evaluate depending on the scan resolution
If consensus is difficult to reach with multi-rater input, it is felt that the lesion is unlikely to represent a true PRL. For single raters, the equivalent subjective experience is a lesion that is simply taking "too long" to evaluate.
Lesion rim has faded to the point of being difficult to distinguish from background noise. This is not part of the formal Brain 2024 criteria but is a common reason for disagreement between raters.
Artifacts can mimic or obscure PRL rims, and commonly include motion (blurring), magnetic dipole artifacts, and susceptibility artifacts from air/bone-tissue interfaces.
PRL rims are typically thin and should not exceed 2 mm in thickness. Thicker rims may indicate a different pathological process or artifact.
Grading criteria based on from Bagnato et al., Brain 2024
These cases are for education purposes only and do not represent the full spectrum of PRL presentations or variations related to scan protocols.

Susceptibility imaging protocol: Philips 3T (software version 5.4.1) "SWIp" protocol, a 3D multi-echo GRE with 6 echoes at TE 9.8-51ms, TR=51ms, 0.6 x 0.6 x 2.0mm voxel acquisition; SENSE factor= 2.5 and 1.29; bandwidth = 217Hz scan time = 353 seconds. Manufacturer-specific post-processing includes phase unwrapping, high-pass filtering and echo combination averaging.
